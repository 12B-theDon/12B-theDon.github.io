About

Thank you for visiting my page! I am an undergraduate student in Smart Mobility Engineering at Inha University. My academic interests began with hands-on robotic system development, including SLAM-based autonomous driving and perception-control integration. This experience led me to explore learning-based robot control, where I implemented imitation and reinforcement learning in simulation environments. More recently, my focus has expanded toward general robot policies, including Vision-Language-Action models, that use the reasoning capabilities of language models to connect visual perception, language understanding, and action generation.

My long-term goal is to develop embodied AI systems that can execute human instructions reliably and safely in complex real-world environments. Based on this interest, I am also interested in bridging the gap between learned policies and real-world deployment by leveraging world models to the robot control. I believe this direction can bring us closer to practical robots that assist people in real-world tasks!

Research Interests
